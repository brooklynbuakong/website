    California's Water Crisis

    The problem concerns people who care about California's water. Water, including the ocean and rivers, are being polluted, ot even from our littering, but from chemical spills. This is one part of the issue with California's water. The other part is the drought. The water supply is decreasing as the climate increases. 75% of the state's precipitation, occurs in Northern California, but Southern California needs 75% of the entire stata's water.

    How Is It Getting Resolved?

    The downside on this subject is that California is barely doing anything to solve this problem. There were no significant infrastructure investments made since the 70s. People must try to expand water storage facilities, which some have achieved. There has been development on California's reservoir space for future temperature changes.

    Water Efficiency

    Institutions regulate the prices for water and how much they should cost depending where it is being sold. For example, water is priced below market value in urban and agricultural areas. Yet, increasing the prices will change water demand, still is not effective. Research studies show that increasing water prices may decrease agricultural water demand. It is very important for people to keep trying to be aware of the water supply and find sustainable funding for it as well. Once the changes have been made, people in California can have reliable water.
